GLOSSARY

For a better understanding of the articles

Terms Signification
Mangaka Manga author, a person who creates Japanese comics, often responsible for the story, drawings and layout.
Shōnen A genre of manga mainly aimed at a young male audience, generally focusing on action, adventure and friendship.
Post-apocalyptic A genre set in the aftermath of a global catastrophe, where civilisation as we know it has been destroyed, leaving behind a devastated world.
One-shot A manga published as a short story, usually a single chapter, which is not part of a series.
Visual story A term that refers to the art of telling a story using images, drawings and graphic compositions rather than relying solely on words.
Vengeance The desire to take revenge or punish someone for a wrong done to them is often a central theme in dramatic and violent stories.
Dark humour A type of humour that tackles serious, dark or taboo subjects in an ironic or cynical way.
Tragedy This is the kind of story that usually ends badly, often linked to unfortunate events or dramatic destinies.
Shōnen Jump A famous Japanese magazine that publishes manga aimed at a young male audience. It is one of the largest and most influential in the shōnen genre.
Philosophically rich A term which means that a work deals with profound questions about life, humanity, morality, etc., often by questioning common values or ideas.
MAPPA A Japanese animation studio renowned for its work on popular series such as ‘Chainsaw Man’.
Reflexive A term for something that invites reflection or introspection on oneself, often used to describe works that explore profound subjects.
Visionary A person with new and original ideas, often ahead of their time, in the creative or artistic field.
